Chefchaouen

The Blue Pearl of Morocco

The first reason my selected audience would come to Chefchaouen is because of the unique aesthetic of the town, with most of its houses painted blue, giving the town an interesting and attractive look.

Marketplaces

Instead of lavish malls that a person would normally see in other destination cities, Chefchaouen boasts its traditional bazaars and street vendors. Most of the merchandise is handmade which allows tourists an authentic and memorable shopping experience.

Food and Drinks

According to the British blog Worldsim Travel, known for its tasty and various dishes, Morocco was classified second in the 2017 ranking of the best gourmand's global destinations. The town offers to its visitors an exceptional culinary experience.

Explore Chefchaouen

Located in the mountains, with its narrow alleys and old architecture, the city offers a quiet and rich cultural and historical experience to visitors. Tourists can walk through the alleys or view the scenic mountain ranges encircling the town.
